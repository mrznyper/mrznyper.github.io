 In the summer of 2016, I was an Application Developer intern for JPMorgan Chase. During my internship, I worked with an intern group to create an internal screen to visualize and attest to all firm-wide positions of a single product. I had the wonderful opportunity to meet frequently with business users and upper management to understand business need and gather requirements. My individual contributions focused on front-end design and development, and data acquisition and formatting. I optimized data queries to return only a few thousand entries instead of half a million, without loss of precision or accuracy in our application.

 Previously, I was also an undergraduate researcher and developer for the CUNY Research Foundation. My research was focused on the statistical analysis and visualization of non-Euclidean treespace. The majority of my work adapted Megan Owen’s previous work into a standalone, cross platform software package. From the very beginning, I designed the program’s behaviour, flow, and appearance to make her codebase much more accessible. My goal was facilitate applications of the program in biomedical imaging and evolutionary biology. Before that, I had also studied computational complexity theory and its applications with Professor Matthew P. Johnson at the CUNY Graduate Center.
